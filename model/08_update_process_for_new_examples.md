# 08 — Update Process for New Investor Examples

Pitch Deck Check should become more useful as new examples are added.

The model should not blindly absorb examples. It should convert them into structured, reusable investor patterns.

## Sources

Examples may come from investment memos, investor blog posts, podcast transcripts, founder interviews, VC interviews, postmortems, fundraising stories, user-provided quotes, or internal analysis.

## Extraction format

### Company
[Company name]

### Source
[Investor, memo, interview, article, etc.]

### Stage
[Seed, Series A, growth, etc.]

### Sector / business model
[Consumer app, B2B SaaS, marketplace, fintech, health, developer tools, etc.]

### Investment rationale
[Why investors said they invested.]

### Relevant deck sections
- [Team]
- [Market]
- [Traction]
- [Product]
- etc.

### Primary investor question answered
[Question]

### Secondary questions answered
- [Question]
- [Question]
- [Question]

### Generalized investor pattern
[Reusable lesson.]

### Specific precedent example
[Company-specific example.]

### Scoring implication
[How future decks should be evaluated.]

### Paid-report usage
[How this can be used in paid guidance.]

### Caveat
[What should not be overgeneralized.]

## Approval workflow

1. Extract structured pattern.
2. Review for accuracy.
3. Generalize the lesson.
4. Add it to the precedent library only if useful.
5. Avoid overfitting to one investor or one company.
6. Tag by section, sector, and business model.
