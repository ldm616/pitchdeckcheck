# 02 — Free Report Rules

The free report is the diagnostic layer. It should be useful enough to build trust and create demand, but it should not reveal the full fix architecture.

## Free report purpose

The free report answers:

- How strong is my deck?
- Which slides are strongest and weakest?
- What investor question is each slide trying to answer?
- Where does the deck underperform?
- How much improvement is possible?

The free report should not answer:

- Exactly what should I add?
- How should I rewrite the slide?
- Which investor examples should I model?
- What is the full underlying rubric?
- What are the detailed scoring weights?

## Free report may include

Deck-level:
- Overall grade
- Investor-readiness summary
- Top deck-level gaps
- Section-by-section grades
- Strongest slides
- Weakest slides
- Missing or underdeveloped sections
- Paywall CTA

Slide-level:
- Slide number
- Inferred slide type
- Grade
- Primary investor question
- 1–2 sentence diagnosis
- Upgrade potential
- Locked upgrade CTA

## Free report should avoid

- full secondary question lists
- exact missing proof categories
- complete fix instructions
- investor precedent examples
- before/after rewrites
- detailed scoring weights
- detailed sub-scores
- named proprietary frameworks
- exhaustive checklists

## Good free language

- “3 targeted additions could raise this slide to B+.”
- “The slide is close, but it needs sharper investor-grade evidence.”
- “The main gap is not polish; it is stronger proof.”
- “Investors may still need clearer evidence that this opportunity can become venture-scale.”
- “This slide explains the idea, but not yet why this company is likely to win.”

## Avoid in free language

- “Add founder-market fit, domain expertise, and GTM advantage.”
- “Use bottom-up TAM, beachhead sizing, and expansion logic.”
- “Add retention, expansion, and CAC payback.”
- “Use LinkedIn, Shopify, and PagerDuty examples.”

## Free slide output template

Slide [number]: [Slide Type]

Grade: [letter grade]

Investor Question:
[Primary investor question]

Diagnosis:
[1–2 sentences explaining what the slide does well or where it under-answers the investor question.]

Upgrade Potential:
[Number] targeted additions could raise this slide to [potential grade].

Upgrade Preview:
[One sentence that describes the opportunity without revealing the full fix architecture.]

Unlock the [number] upgrades:
Get the exact additions, suggested wording, and relevant investor-backed examples.
