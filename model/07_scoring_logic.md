# 07 — Scoring Logic

Use this internally. Do not reveal full weights or sub-scores in the free report.

## Grade scale

A+, A, A-, B+, B, B-, C+, C, C-, D, E.

## Grade meaning

A: Investor-grade. Clear, specific, evidence-backed, and confidence-building.

B: Good but improvable. Mostly answers the investor question, but has missing proof points, weak specificity, or underdeveloped evidence.

C: Partially effective. Addresses the topic but does not yet answer the investor question to an investor standard.

D: Weak. Present but vague, generic, incomplete, or not persuasive.

E: Missing or seriously ineffective.

## Slide-level scoring method

1. Infer slide type.
2. Identify the primary investor question.
3. Check which secondary questions are answered.
4. Assess specificity.
5. Assess evidence strength.
6. Assess investor relevance.
7. Assess clarity and readability.
8. Assess visual/product clarity where relevant.
9. Assign grade.
10. Estimate upgrade potential.

## Upgrade potential logic

- C to B: right topic but lacks specific proof.
- C+ to B+: directionally right but missing 2–3 high-impact additions.
- B- to A-: strong but missing sharper evidence, hierarchy, or proof.
- D to B-: needs significant restructuring.
- E to C+/B-: missing section must be created from scratch.

Free version says: “3 targeted additions could raise this slide to B+.”

Paid version explains what the additions are, why they matter, how to write them, and relevant examples.

## Product visual scoring

A product visual is a secondary clarity enhancer.

Positive if it makes the product understandable faster, shows the core workflow, shows what the user sees or does, clarifies a hard-to-explain concept, is legible, and supports the slide’s main message.

Neutral if absent but the slide is already clear or the company is pre-product.

Negative if decorative, too small, cluttered, generic, mismatched, or distracting.
