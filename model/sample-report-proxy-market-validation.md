## Purpose

This sample report demonstrates how Pitch Deck Check should evaluate an early-stage deck that has limited direct product traction but credible proxy market validation.

The sample shows how adjacent behavior, imperfect substitutes, competitor activity and marketplace signals can support an early investment thesis.

It also shows why proxy validation does not fully replace direct validation from the company's own customers, users or marketplace participants.

## Sample Report

# Pitch Deck Check Report

Company: StayNest

Deck: StayNest Seed Deck.pdf

Generated: Sample Report

This report evaluates the pitch deck as presented. It does not predict fundraising success or judge the company's ultimate potential outside the deck.

---

## Context Summary

Detected Company Context: Idea / Pre-Product

Context Confidence: Medium

Intended Investor Audience: Pre-seed investors or angel investors

Target Raise: Not provided

Evaluation Note: The deck appears to be an early marketplace concept. Because the company has limited direct product traction, the evaluation places more weight on problem clarity, market validation, founder insight, supply/demand logic and the credibility of the first-market wedge.

---

## Overall Grade: B

StayNest presents a clear and credible early-stage marketplace thesis. The deck does a strong job showing that the underlying customer behavior already exists through imperfect substitutes and adjacent marketplaces.

Primary Constraint: Direct Validation

What this means: The deck makes a credible case that the market opportunity and value gap are real, but investors will still want stronger evidence that StayNest itself can convert this behavior into direct supply, demand, usage or revenue.

| Dimension | Score | What it means |
|---|---:|---|
| Completeness | 4/5 — Strong | Most core early-stage investor questions are addressed, especially the customer problem, current alternatives, market behavior and initial wedge. |
| Clarity | 4/5 — Strong | The deck clearly explains who the marketplace serves and why current alternatives are imperfect. |
| Brevity | 4/5 — Strong | The deck is concise and mostly avoids unnecessary detail. |

The deck is clear, efficient and logically sequenced. The main limitation is not communication quality. The main limitation is that the strongest validation is indirect rather than generated by StayNest's own product.

---

## Investment Case as Presented

| Area | Assessment | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| Opportunity Strength | Promising but Under-Supported | The deck provides credible proxy market validation showing that customers already use imperfect alternatives, but the size and urgency of the opportunity still need stronger direct proof. |
| Execution Credibility | Mixed | The founders appear to understand the market wedge, but the deck needs more evidence that they can create both sides of the marketplace. |
| Investor Fit | Mixed | The deck may fit pre-seed investors or angels comfortable with marketplace risk, but it is not yet strong enough for investors who require direct traction or proven liquidity. |

Market validation supports the opportunity. The deck shows that customers already engage in adjacent behavior, use imperfect substitutes and participate in comparable markets. That evidence helps investors believe the value gap is real. However, the deck still needs more direct validation that StayNest can attract supply, generate demand and create repeat usage.

---

## Primary Diagnosis

The deck makes a credible early case that the market opportunity is real by showing adjacent customer behavior, but it still needs direct validation that customers will adopt this company's product.

This is an evidence issue, not primarily a writing issue.

The deck already explains the thesis clearly. The next version should prove that the company can convert existing behavior into its own marketplace activity.

---

## What Investors May Believe

Investors may believe that:

- The company is addressing a real customer behavior rather than inventing a market from scratch.
- Customers already seek this outcome through imperfect alternatives.
- Existing substitute behavior suggests both supply and demand may be present.
- The founders understand why current alternatives are fragmented, inefficient or poorly trusted.
- The initial wedge is specific enough to test.

These are meaningful strengths for an early-stage marketplace deck.

---

## What Investors May Question

Investors may ask:

- Will users switch from existing substitutes to StayNest?
- Can StayNest create enough supply and demand in the same initial market?
- What evidence shows hosts, suppliers or listing participants will join the platform?
- What evidence shows buyers, guests or demand-side users will transact?
- How will the company solve trust, liquidity and cold-start problems?
- Is the opportunity large enough beyond the initial wedge?
- What makes the marketplace defensible if the behavior already exists elsewhere?

The strongest investor question is not whether the behavior exists. The deck provides credible proxy evidence that it does. The stronger question is whether StayNest can capture and organize that behavior better than current alternatives.

---

## Priority Improvements

1. Add direct market validation.
   - Why it matters: Proxy behavior shows the market may exist, but investors need evidence that users will adopt this company's product.
   - What to add: Customer interviews, waitlist quality, signed supply interest, demand-side discovery, prototype usage, pilot results or early marketplace commitments.
   - Issue type: Evidence / Market Validation.

2. Prove the first-market wedge.
   - Why it matters: Marketplaces usually need density in a narrow segment before they can scale.
   - What to add: A specific launch market, target user segment, supply acquisition plan and demand-generation plan.
   - Issue type: Go-to-Market / Execution Credibility.

3. Explain the liquidity path.
   - Why it matters: Investors need to believe both sides of the marketplace can be created close enough together to produce transactions.
   - What to add: Supply targets, demand targets, expected conversion steps and the sequence for reaching first repeat usage.
   - Issue type: Execution Credibility.

4. Strengthen the expansion case.
   - Why it matters: Proxy validation supports the initial opportunity, but investors need to understand how the company becomes large.
   - What to add: Adjacent segments, market expansion logic, repeatable city or category launch model and long-term market size support.
   - Issue type: Opportunity Strength.

## Slide-Level Feedback

### Cover

Investor Decision: What does this company do, who is it for and why should I care?

Assessment: Mostly answered.

What works: The deck quickly communicates that StayNest is building a marketplace around underused short-term spaces and unmet demand.

What is missing: The one-sentence description could be more specific about the initial customer segment and use case.

Recommended improvement: Add a sharper positioning line that names the first target user, the supply source and the customer outcome.

Issue type: Clarity.

---

### Problem

Investor Decision: Is there a meaningful value gap that customers are motivated to close?

Assessment: Strong.

What works: The deck explains that customers already search for flexible short-term options but rely on fragmented, inconsistent or low-trust alternatives.

What is missing: The deck needs stronger evidence that the pain is urgent enough to make users switch.

Recommended improvement: Add customer quotes, search behavior, failed substitute examples or discovery evidence showing frustration with current alternatives.

Issue type: Evidence / Market Validation.

---

### Current Alternatives

Investor Decision: How do customers solve this today, and why are current approaches insufficient?

Assessment: Strong.

What works: The deck uses existing substitute behavior to show that supply and demand already exist. This is useful proxy market validation.

What is missing: The deck should more clearly explain which alternative is most important to replace first.

Recommended improvement: Identify the highest-friction substitute and explain why StayNest can deliver a better experience.

Issue type: Competition / Opportunity Strength.

---

### Market Validation

Investor Decision: What evidence shows customers already want the underlying outcome?

Assessment: Promising but indirect.

What works: The deck cites adjacent behavior, substitute listings and comparable marketplace activity to show that the behavior already exists.

What is missing: The evidence is mostly external. The deck does not yet show enough direct validation from StayNest's own target users.

Recommended improvement: Add first-party evidence such as interviews, waitlists, pilot conversations, supply commitments or early prototype engagement.

Issue type: Evidence.

---

### Market Opportunity

Investor Decision: Is this opportunity large enough to support the intended investor outcome?

Assessment: Partially answered.

What works: The deck identifies a plausible initial market and points to adjacent activity as evidence that the broader category exists.

What is missing: The expansion path from first wedge to larger market is not yet fully supported.

Recommended improvement: Show how the initial wedge expands into adjacent customer segments, geographies or categories.

Issue type: Opportunity Strength.

---

### Go-to-Market

Investor Decision: Can the company repeatedly and efficiently acquire customers?

Assessment: Under-supported.

What works: The deck identifies a logical starting segment.

What is missing: The deck does not yet explain how supply and demand will be created in the same market at the same time.

Recommended improvement: Add a launch sequence that shows how StayNest will recruit supply, generate demand, create first transactions and learn from early usage.

Issue type: Execution Credibility.

---

### Team

Investor Decision: Why is this team more likely to succeed than other capable teams?

Assessment: Partially answered.

What works: The founders appear to have relevant insight into the customer problem and marketplace behavior.

What is missing: The deck needs stronger evidence of execution ability, marketplace experience, domain access or early recruiting advantage.

Recommended improvement: Add founder-market fit, relevant operating experience, unique access or specific evidence that the team can create liquidity.

Issue type: Execution Credibility.

---

### Funding

Investor Decision: Does the financing plan create a credible path to the next major value-creating milestone?

Assessment: Under-supported.

What works: The deck implies a pre-seed stage and a need to validate the marketplace.

What is missing: The target raise, use of funds and milestone plan are not specific enough.

Recommended improvement: State the raise amount, intended runway, use of funds and the validation milestones the company expects to reach before the next financing.

Issue type: Completeness / Investor Fit.

---

## Suggested Next Steps

1. Turn proxy validation into direct validation.
   Keep the adjacent-behavior evidence, but add first-party proof from the company's own target users.

2. Define the first wedge more tightly.
   Choose the narrowest market where supply and demand can be created quickly enough to prove liquidity.

3. Add marketplace cold-start logic.
   Explain how the company will sequence supply acquisition, demand generation and trust-building.

4. Clarify the financing plan.
   Add the target raise, use of funds and milestones that prove the company is ready for the next stage.

5. Re-run the analysis after revision.
   The next version should test whether the primary constraint shifts from direct validation to execution credibility or investor fit.
